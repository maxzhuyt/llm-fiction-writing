# Story Idea 084

**Words:** poss, handgun, tooth, mess, usher, anomaly, flores, homeowner, aerial, stepping, automobile, observer, crushing, rainbow, algorithm, bank, breathing, prism, attent, simmer

---

**Form: Annotated home inspection report with increasingly desperate marginalia**

A forensic accountant purchasing her first home receives the standard inspection document, but the previous inspector's handwritten notes in the margins describe rooms that don't exist in the floor plan—a nursery behind the water heater, a "breathing wall" in the basement, a door that "only appears when the prism light hits the kitchen at 4:47 PM." As she cross-references county records, she discovers seventeen homeowners have owned this property since 1987, each for exactly eleven months, and the inspector who wrote these notes vanished mid-sentence during his final annotation: "The tooth marks on the joists are not from termites. They are from—" She has ten months to solve what he couldn't, and the algorithm she's built to predict the pattern suggests the house doesn't consume its owners randomly; it's selecting for a specific trait she's only now realizing she possesses.
